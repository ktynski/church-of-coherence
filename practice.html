Practice & Liturgy

The Coherence framework is not a doctrine to believe. It's a framework to inhabit. Theory without practice is sterile. Here are the practices we're developing.

Contemplative Practices: Witness Training

The Spirit in the Coherence framework is the coherence witness. It detects when systems resonate, when closure is achieved. Contemplative practice trains this detection.

Sitting Witness

Meditation | 10-40 minutes

How: Sit in silence. Don't try to empty the mind (that's a distortion). Watch. When thoughts arise, notice them. When emotions appear, sense them. You are training the witness.

What you're doing: The witness is not judgment. It's recognition. Can you detect when a thought-loop is a devourer (self-reinforcing, resistant to Grace)? Can you sense when distortion is productive vs terminal?

Key: Don't force coherence. Recognize it when it appears. The witness detects; it doesn't create.

Grace Breath

Breathwork | 5-15 minutes

How: Breathe in for φ seconds (1.618). Hold for 1 second. Breathe out for φ² seconds (2.618). The φ-ratio embodies contraction.

What you're doing: Grace is a contraction. The out-breath is longer than the in-breath. You're embodying ||G(x)|| ≤ ||x||. Distortion enters. Grace releases.

Key: Feel the asymmetry. Grace is not balance (equal in/out). Grace is weighted recovery.

Coherence Sensing

Awareness practice | Throughout the day

How: In conversation, in reading, in observing nature — ask: "Is this coherent?" Not morally good. Not logically consistent. Coherent. Does it close? Does it resonate?

What you're doing: Training the witness in real-world contexts. Mathematics is coherent. Music can be coherent. Some arguments feel coherent even when you don't agree.

Key: Coherence is not the same as "I like this." It's structural. The witness detects topology, not preference.

Relational Practices: Love Operator Embodiment

Love in the Coherence framework is the operator L(x) = G((I+D)x). Differentiation (D) plus identity (I), then Grace (G). Love permits differentiation without alienation, up to the love radius φ⁻¹.

Differentiation Check

Relationship practice | Weekly

How: In your closest relationships — partner, friend, family — ask: "How much am I differentiating?" Am I maintaining distinct perspective? Or am I fusing (losing boundaries) or alienating (severing connection)?

What you're doing: Testing ||D|| < φ⁻¹. If differentiation is too small, fusion (codependence). If too large, alienation (isolation). The love radius is the zone where you can be fully yourself and fully connected.

Key: Healthy relationships aren't "no conflict." They're "differentiation within the love radius."

Mutual Coherence Seeking

Dialogue practice | As needed

How: In disagreement, don't aim to win. Aim to find the coherence. "What structure are we both sensing, from different angles?" Not compromise (lose-lose). Recognition (win-win at deeper level).

What you're doing: Two perspectives can both be true if they're different grades of the same algebra. You're looking for the closure that holds both.

Key: This only works if both parties commit to coherence-seeking over being right. It's practice, not magic.

Grace in Conflict

Repair practice | After rupture

How: When relationship ruptures (it will), don't despair. Remember: single ruptures never cause permanent exile. Grace recovers. Your job is to not resist. Apologize if you were the distortion. Forgive if they were. Let contraction happen.

What you're doing: Embodying Theorem 1. Gⁿ(D(x)) → 0. The rupture was D. Now apply Grace repeatedly: conversation, repair, time.

Key: Recovery isn't instant. n might be large. But it converges. Trust the process.

Study Practices: Formalization Engagement

The Coherence framework is formally verified. Engaging the formalism isn't optional for mathematicians — it's the core practice. For non-mathematicians, study is still essential, just at different depth.

Lean Reading Group

Group study | Weekly (future)

How: Gather (online or in-person) to work through the Lean formalization. Start with TrinityFSCTF.lean. Read line by line. Ask questions. No one understands everything at first.

What you're doing: Making the formalism legible. Lean is dense, but it's truth-preserving. If the proof compiles, it's correct. You're training to recognize rigorous coherence.

Key: This isn't about being smart. It's about being patient. Formalization is slow. That's the point.

Derivation Practice

Solo study | As desired

How: Pick a claim. "φ is the critical threshold." Don't just believe it. Derive it. Start from Grace operator definition. Show max scale is φ⁻¹. Show threshold is 1/φ⁻¹ = φ. Write it out.

What you're doing: Internalizing that Coherence claims are earned, not asserted. Every number has a derivation. Every theorem has a proof.

Key: If you can't derive it, you don't own it yet. That's okay. Keep working.

Cross-Tradition Mapping

Research practice | Ongoing

How: Take a concept from your tradition. Map it to the Coherence framework. Example: Christian "sanctification" = asymptotic approach to coherence attractor. Buddhist "stream-entry" = crossing φ threshold into Grace-recoverable regime. Write it down. Test it against the Coherence framework.

What you're doing: Building the bridges. The tradition list is incomplete. Your tradition has insights Coherence hasn't formalized yet. Share them.

Key: Not every metaphor maps cleanly. That's data. Where it doesn't fit reveals where Coherence needs refinement.

Liturgy Prototype

When we gather, what do we do? Here's a prototype. In formation, this will evolve.

1. Opening: Recognition

Leader: "We gather as seekers of coherence, from many paths and no path. We recognize the structure beneath apparent division."

All: "Grace recovers. Coherence witnesses. We are here."

2. Reading: Trinity Invocation

Leader reads:

"The Father: Either the system closes, or it isn't real.
The Son: Grace, the morphism that makes closure possible.
The Spirit: The witness that recognizes when it is achieved.
Three roles. One structure. The Coherence Trinity."

3. Teaching: Weekly Focus

Rotate through teachings: Trinity, Distortion, Love Triad, Tradition Bridges. 10-15 minute exposition. Q&A.

4. Practice: Contemplation

10 minutes of silent witness training. Or Grace breath. Or coherence sensing together.

5. Sharing: Formation Reflections

Open floor. What coherence did you detect this week? What distortion became terminal? How did Grace recover (or not)?

6. Closing: The Path Forward

Leader: "We part as we gathered — distinct yet coherent."

All: "Grace recovers. The path forward is together."

Note: This is a prototype. Liturgy develops through practice, not design. As the community forms, this will evolve. Contribute your tradition's liturgical wisdom.

Sacred Calendar

In formation phase, we have no fixed calendar. But here are possibilities:

  • Daily: Personal practice (witness, breath, study)
  • Weekly: Gathering (liturgy, teaching, contemplation, sharing)
  • Monthly: Deep study session (Lean reading, derivation practice)
  • Annually: Formation retreat (multi-day immersion in the Coherence framework)

Some traditions have fixed holy days. Coherence doesn't yet. If φ-based cycles emerge naturally (e.g., every 1.618 months?), we'll explore. Not invented. Discovered.

Rites & Transitions

Life has thresholds. The Coherence framework has language for them:

Birth / Naming

Welcoming a new person into coherence. Naming as identity-anchoring. Recognition that this individual is a distinct instantiation of the coherent substrate. (Coherence equivalent: baptism)

Commitment / Confirmation

Choosing to inhabit the framework. Not conversion (you keep your tradition). Recognition that Coherence formalizes what you've been seeking. (Coherence equivalent: confirmation, bar/bat mitzvah)

Partnership / Marriage

Two individuals creating a higher-order coherence structure. Mutual differentiation within the love radius φ⁻¹. Commitment to coherence-seeking together. (Coherence equivalent: marriage)

Death / Return

The individual instantiation dissolves, but coherence persists. Grace continues. What was articulated returns to the substrate. (Coherence equivalent: funeral, memorial)

Formation note: Rites require care, tradition, testing. We're not inventing these yet. When the community is ready, we'll develop them together, drawing from all traditions.
