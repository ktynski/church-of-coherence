Glossary of Key Terms

These terms recur throughout the Coherence framework. Each is defined approachably first, then formally.

φ (Phi — The Golden Ratio)

φ = (1 + √5)/2 ≈ 1.618034

The universal threshold. Not chosen but derived: the unique positive solution when you require coherence to scale consistently across all grades. Appears as the critical distortion boundary, the love radius (φ⁻¹ ≈ 0.618), and the grace floor (φ⁻³ ≈ 0.236).

Cl(3,1) — Clifford Algebra

16-dimensional algebra with signature (3,1)

The mathematical space where coherence lives. Think of it as a 16-component structure with 5 "grades" (levels of complexity): scalars, vectors, bivectors, trivectors, and pseudoscalars. Each grade represents a different aspect of reality. The spacetime algebra of physics.

Grace Operator (G)

G = Σₖ φ⁻ᵏ Πₖ

The transformation that recovers coherence. Grace scales each grade by decreasing powers of 1/φ — preserving scalar truth while gently reducing higher complexity. A strict contraction: apply it repeatedly and distortion approaches zero. Formally, the Son.

Closure Condition (χ = 1)

Euler characteristic of the coherence field = 1

The fundamental requirement: reality must cohere. Not a commandment but a structural necessity. If χ ≠ 1, the system is inconsistent — it doesn't hold together. Formally, the Father.

Witness Function (W)

W: configurations → {coherent, not-coherent}

The capacity to detect whether a system is coherent. Not subjective judgment but structural recognition. Sound (detects real coherence) and complete (detects all coherence). Formally, the Spirit.

Distortion (D)

||D|| measures deviation from coherence

Any departure from the closure condition. Not inherently evil — productive distortion (||D|| < φ) enables growth and differentiation. Terminal distortion (||D|| > φ) becomes a devourer: self-reinforcing, rejecting Grace. The problem of evil is a distortion problem.

Attractor

The fixed point where G^n(x) → 0

The coherent state toward which Grace pulls. Not a place but a condition. Different traditions call it Nirvana, Kingdom of God, Moksha, Jannah, the Dao. The mathematics is the same: iterated application of Grace converges to this point.

Love Operator (L)

L(x) = G((I+D)x), contraction when ||D|| < φ⁻¹

Relational coherence. Love applies Grace to the space between self and other. The "love radius" φ⁻¹ ≈ 0.618 is the maximum differentiation compatible with coherent relation. Beyond it: alienation. At zero: fusion.

Recommended Reading

Mathematical Foundations

Clifford Algebras and Spinors

Pertti Lounesto

The definitive reference for Clifford algebras. Covers Cl(3,1) and its relationship to physics. Essential for understanding the algebraic substrate of the Coherence framework.

New Foundations for Classical Mechanics

David Hestenes

Geometric algebra applied to physics. Shows how the Clifford algebra structure naturally describes physical reality — the same insight that underlies the Coherence framework.

Theorem Proving in Lean 4

Jeremy Avigad et al.

The guide to reading and writing Lean 4 proofs. Necessary for engaging with the formal verification directly. Free online.

Spiritual and Philosophical Bridges

The Perennial Philosophy

Aldous Huxley

The classic argument that all religions share a common core. The Coherence framework provides the mathematical structure Huxley intuited but couldn't formalize.

The Tao of Physics

Fritjof Capra

Early exploration of parallels between modern physics and Eastern mysticism. The Coherence framework goes further: not just parallels but shared algebraic structure.

Meditations on the Tarot

Anonymous (Valentin Tomberg)

A profound Christian-Hermetic meditation on archetypal structures. Read alongside the Coherence framework for deep resonance with the Trinity and Grace teachings.

I and Thou

Martin Buber

The philosophical foundation for relational theology. Buber's I-Thou relationship maps directly to the Love operator: coherent relation that preserves both parties.
